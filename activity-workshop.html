Game Development Workshop

Educational Activity • Teaching & Mentoring • 2024

Teaching Unity Mentoring Community

Overview

Organized and conducted a series of game development workshops for beginners at Srinakharinwirot University. The workshops aimed to introduce students to game development fundamentals, Unity engine basics, and programming concepts through hands-on projects.

Workshop Details:
Duration: 6 sessions (3 hours each)
Participants: 30+ students
Format: Hands-on coding with live demonstrations
Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Curriculum

The workshop series covered essential game development topics:

  • Week 1: Introduction to Unity Interface & Game Objects
  • Week 2: C# Programming Basics for Games
  • Week 3: Player Movement & Input Systems
  • Week 4: Collision Detection & Game Physics
  • Week 5: UI Systems & Game States
  • Week 6: Final Project - Create Your First Complete Game

Teaching Approach

Focused on learning by doing - each session included live coding demonstrations followed by hands-on exercises. Students built progressively more complex games, starting with simple mechanics and gradually adding features.

  • Interactive live coding sessions with real-time Q&A
  • Hands-on exercises with immediate feedback
  • Code review sessions to improve best practices
  • One-on-one mentoring during practice time
  • Resource sharing and continued support after workshops

Student Projects

By the end of the workshop, students created their own games including 2D platformers, simple puzzle games, and arcade-style shooters. Many participants continued game development and joined game jams afterward.

Impact:
  • 85% of participants completed all workshop sessions
  • 20+ students went on to create their own game projects
  • 5 participants joined competitive game jams
  • Formed a student game development community that continues to meet regularly

What I Learned

Teaching game development to beginners taught me how to break down complex concepts into digestible pieces and explain technical topics in accessible ways. It also improved my own understanding of Unity fundamentals and best practices.
